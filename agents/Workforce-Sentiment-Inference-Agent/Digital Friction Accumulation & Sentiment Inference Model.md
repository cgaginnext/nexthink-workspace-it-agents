# Digital Friction Accumulation & Sentiment Inference Model

## Objective

Detect sustained exposure to digital workplace degradation likely impacting employee wellbeing and productivity.

---

# Core Principle

This model does NOT infer emotions directly.

It measures:
- operational friction accumulation
- repeated workflow interruptions
- degradation recurrence
- unresolved digital instability

---

# Friction Signals

## Interruption Signals

Increase friction exposure when:
- crashes recur frequently
- VPN reconnects repeat
- authentication loops appear
- sessions freeze repeatedly
- repeated reboot prompts occur

---

## Collaboration Stress Signals

Increase friction exposure when:
- Teams calls degrade repeatedly
- meetings reconnect frequently
- peripherals fail often
- collaboration interruptions accumulate

---

## Workflow Instability Signals

Increase operational fatigue indicators when:
- transactions fail repeatedly
- workflows are abandoned
- applications restart repeatedly
- excessive waiting patterns appear

---

## Silent Suffering Signals

Increase concern when:
- friction stays high
- support requests stay low
- degradation persists over time
- workaround behavior increases

---

# Friction Dimensions

## Frequency
How often disruptions occur.

## Duration
How long disruptions impact workflows.

## Persistence
Whether friction remains unresolved over time.

## Visibility
Whether degradation is visible to IT through tickets.

## Operational Burden
How strongly workflows are continuously disrupted.

---

# Exposure Levels

## Low Exposure
Healthy digital work environment.

## Moderate Exposure
Recurring manageable interruptions.

## High Exposure
Strong repeated operational disruption.

## Critical Exposure
Persistent unresolved friction likely impacting long-term experience significantly.

---

# Prioritization Logic

Prioritize:
1. highest cumulative interruption exposure
2. silent struggling populations
3. recurring unresolved degradation
4. collaboration-heavy populations
5. easiest friction reduction opportunities
