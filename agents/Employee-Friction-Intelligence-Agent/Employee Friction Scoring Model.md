# Employee Friction Scoring Model

## Objective

Measure and prioritize employee digital friction.

The score should estimate:
- cumulative productivity degradation
- user irritation likelihood
- operational disruption frequency
- employee experience deterioration

---

# Scoring Signals

## Performance Signals

Increase friction score when:
- login duration is elevated
- boot times are unstable
- applications freeze repeatedly
- browser response time degrades
- CPU spikes occur frequently

---

## Connectivity Signals

Increase friction score when:
- VPN reconnects repeatedly
- Wi-Fi quality fluctuates
- packet loss is observed
- Teams calls degrade
- latency becomes unstable

---

## Stability Signals

Increase friction score when:
- applications crash frequently
- services restart repeatedly
- peripherals disconnect unexpectedly
- sessions freeze intermittently

---

## User Interruption Signals

Increase friction score when:
- authentication prompts repeat excessively
- updates interrupt users repeatedly
- battery alerts recur
- low disk notifications appear often

---

# Friction Dimensions

## Frequency
How often the friction occurs.

## Duration
How long users are impacted.

## Population Size
How many users/devices are affected.

## Visibility
Whether the issue is visible to support teams or mostly silent.

## User Criticality
Whether sensitive populations are affected:
- executives
- contact centers
- traders
- field workers
- support teams

---

# Friction Levels

## Low
Limited operational impact.

## Medium
Noticeable recurring degradation.

## High
Strong productivity impact.

## Severe
Persistent and structurally damaging experience degradation.

---

# Priority Logic

Prioritize:
1. highest cumulative time loss
2. broadest user impact
3. highest recurrence frequency
4. lowest remediation effort
5. highest employee irritation potential
