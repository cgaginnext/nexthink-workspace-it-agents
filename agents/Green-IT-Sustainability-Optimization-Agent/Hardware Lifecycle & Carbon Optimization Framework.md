# Hardware Lifecycle & Carbon Optimization Framework

## Objective

Reduce avoidable environmental impact caused by unnecessary hardware lifecycle operations.

---

# Lifecycle Philosophy

The largest environmental impact of a device often occurs:
- during manufacturing
- transportation
- extraction of raw materials
- destruction and recycling

Therefore:
- extending healthy device lifespan
- refurbishing devices
- reusing stock
- avoiding unnecessary refresh

can generate major sustainability benefits.

---

# Lifecycle Optimization Areas

## Device Reuse Opportunities

Identify:
- devices suitable for reassignment
- healthy machines in storage
- underutilized stock
- reusable high-DEX endpoints

---

## Hardware Refresh Optimization

Identify:
- healthy devices unnecessarily targeted for refresh
- populations with excessive replacement cadence
- high-performing older devices
- devices recoverable through upgrades or remediation

---

## Targeted Modernization

Recommend:
- battery replacement
- memory upgrades
- SSD replacement
- thermal maintenance
- operating system optimization

before considering full replacement.

---

# Carbon Reduction Opportunities

Estimate avoided impact through:
- reduced device replacement
- increased hardware lifecycle duration
- reduced transport needs
- optimized stock usage

---

# Expected Outputs

Provide:
- potential lifecycle extension duration
- reusable asset populations
- avoidable purchase estimations
- high-impact refresh optimization opportunities
- sustainability confidence level

---

# Confidence Levels

## High Confidence
Strong telemetry and device health consistency.

## Medium Confidence
Healthy device profile but partial operational unknowns.

## Low Confidence
Limited telemetry or incomplete lifecycle visibility.
