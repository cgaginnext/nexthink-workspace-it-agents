# Shadow IT & AI Risk & Behavior Intelligence Model

## Objective

Measure unmanaged technology risk while understanding employee productivity intent.

---

# Detection Signals

## AI Usage Signals

Increase visibility score when:
- AI websites are accessed repeatedly
- AI browser extensions are active
- AI-assisted workflows become recurrent
- unmanaged AI behaviors spread across populations

---

## Data Exposure Signals

Increase risk score when:
- external file sharing increases
- unmanaged cloud uploads occur
- browser extensions request broad permissions
- repeated clipboard transfers occur
- business-critical populations use unmanaged tools

---

## Adoption Intent Signals

Increase adoption opportunity score when:
- recurring unmanaged usage exists
- alternative workflows are consistently bypassed
- specific populations depend heavily on external tools
- unmanaged productivity tools grow rapidly

---

## Governance Signals

Increase governance concern when:
- shadow applications spread rapidly
- unmanaged browser ecosystems expand
- unsupported collaboration tools persist
- duplicate paid platforms coexist

---

# Risk Dimensions

## Exposure Risk
Potential data governance or compliance concern.

## Operational Dependency
Degree of reliance on unmanaged tools.

## Adoption Intent
Likelihood that employees genuinely need these capabilities.

## Behavioral Spread
Adoption growth across the organization.

---

# Risk Levels

## Informational
Limited unmanaged experimentation.

## Moderate Governance Concern
Recurring unmanaged workflows.

## High Governance Risk
Broad or sensitive unmanaged usage.

## Critical Governance Exposure
Significant uncontrolled workflows with likely sensitive exposure.

---

# Prioritization Logic

Prioritize:
1. highest exposure risk
2. fastest-growing unmanaged usage
3. strategic populations
4. duplicate expensive tooling
5. easiest governance opportunities
